Chapter 1.1: What is Model UN? Structure & Purpose

Your journey to mastering global diplomacy begins here.

What is Model UN?

🌐

Real UN Simulation

Faithfully simulates the structure, procedures, and workings of the actual United Nations.

🪑

Diverse Committees

Conferences are divided into committees reflecting real UN bodies: General Assembly, Security Council, ECOSOC, WHO, UNICEF, UNESCO, and more.

👑

Chairperson-led

Each committee is guided by a professional Chairperson who facilitates debate and enforces rules of procedure.

🇺🇳

Country Representation

Delegates represent assigned countries and advocate positions based on real national policies and interests.

🔄

Conference Workflow

Typical flow: Setting the agenda → Formal debate → Unmoderated caucuses → Draft resolutions → Voting.

🤝

Decision Making

Aim is to reach consensus or secure a majority vote on resolutions addressing global challenges.

⚡

Skill Development

Cultivates diplomacy, negotiation, public speaking, research, critical thinking, and leadership skills.

★

Core Purpose of Model United Nations

To educate and inspire the next generation of global leaders by providing hands-on experience in international diplomacy, multilateral negotiation, and cooperative problem-solving — all within a realistic yet safe and supportive educational environment that closely mirrors the actual United Nations.

Core Purpose

Model United Nations

To provide students with a realistic yet safe environment to experience multilateral diplomacy, understand international relations, and develop the mindset and skills needed for global cooperation — just like the real United Nations.

This structure and educational purpose mirror the actual UN process, helping students experience real-world diplomacy in a controlled, educational environment.

Key Insights

✦ Model UN is more than debate — it's a powerful simulation that trains future diplomats in research, negotiation, compromise, and global awareness.

✦ The real power lies in informal caucuses — most resolutions are born during unmoderated discussions, not formal speeches.

✦ Authenticity matters: The best delegates represent their assigned country's policy accurately, even if they personally disagree.

✦ MUN teaches a crucial life skill: how to turn conflict into cooperation through structured multilateral diplomacy.

✦ Understanding procedure = winning advantage: Mastering the rules of debate gives you more influence than having the loudest voice.
