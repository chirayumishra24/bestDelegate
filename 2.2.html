Chapter 2.2: Position Papers and Opening Statements

Position Papers Your Strategic Blueprint for Committee Dominance

Model UN Strategy: How to Write a Winning Position Paper

1
📋

Format & Header

1 page per topic (max 2 in some conferences)
Include: Committee • Topic • Country • Delegate name & school
Standard formatting: 12 pt font, 1" margins, single-spaced

2
🧱

Core Structure

1. Background & current status of the issue
2. Past actions (UN resolutions, treaties, national efforts)
3. Your country's policy & involvement
4. Realistic, policy-aligned proposed solutions

3
✍️

Writing & Style

Use formal diplomatic language (third person)
Cite sources properly (UN docs, official statements)
Represent only your assigned country's position — no personal views

4
🎯

Strategic Impact

Builds credibility before debate begins
Guides your speeches, negotiations & alliances
Acts as your "silent ambassador" throughout committee

🏆

Why It Matters

A well-crafted position paper doesn't just summarize policy — it positions you as a serious, prepared delegate from the very first session.

“Your position paper is your silent ambassador — it speaks for you before you even stand up.”

Key Learning Points

✦ Position papers are your strategic blueprint — concise, formal documents that showcase deep research and your country’s official stance.

✦ Opening statements are your first impression — short, confident speeches that introduce your position and invite collaboration.

Mastering both tools establishes your credibility, helps build alliances, and positions you as a prepared and influential delegate.
