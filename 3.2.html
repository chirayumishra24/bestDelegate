Chapter 3.2: Argumentation, Rebuttal, Active Listening & Diplomacy

Argumentation

From: Model UN Masterclass 3/4

FOUNDATION

Evidence-Backed Arguments

Build strong, coherent arguments supported by concrete evidence (data, treaties, historical facts) that strictly align with your country's official stance.

 
TOOL 1

SEEC Framework

State your claim → give a clear Example → provide deep Explanation → close with Conclusion that reinforces your position.

TOOL 2

PEEL Framework

Make a sharp Point → support it with solid Evidence → offer clear Explanation → Link it back to your overall stance or the next argument.

 
FINAL GOAL

Solution-Oriented Focus

Always frame arguments around actionable solutions — clearly show why your proposal matters, why it is realistic, and why it is the correct path forward for the committee.

Strong argumentation turns facts into influence — evidence + structure + solution-focus wins debates and shapes resolutions.

Rebuttal

 
 
 
 
R1

Tactful Challenge

Challenge opponents’ arguments logically — focus on flaws, weak evidence, or contradictions without aggression.

R2

Calm & Factual Tone

Always maintain a composed, evidence-based tone — never resort to personal attacks or emotional language.

R3

Sandwich Method

Acknowledge valid points → politely criticize weakness → offer your stronger alternative (keeps diplomacy intact).

R4

Strategic Questioning

Ask precise, targeted questions that expose contradictions — do it respectfully to avoid alienating potential allies.

Effective rebuttal is not about winning arguments — it is about exposing weaknesses diplomatically while strengthening your own position.

Active Listening

From: HOW TO WIN MUN BEST DELEGATE (short and sweet)

👂

Active Listening Core

The silent superpower

Full Focus & Clarification

Fully concentrate on speakers • ask clarifying questions • summarize points to confirm understanding

Builds Trust & Common Ground

Creates trust • uncovers shared interests • enables smarter, more targeted responses

Adapt & Collaborate

Lets you instantly adapt negotiation tactics • spot alliance opportunities • foster real committee collaboration

 
Active listening is not passive silence — it is the strategic superpower that turns good delegates into the best delegates.

Key Learning Points

Strong argumentation requires evidence-based, structured points (SEEC/PEEL) that align with your country’s stance.

Effective rebuttal is respectful and logical — never personal.

Active listening and diplomacy are the foundation of trust, collaboration, and successful negotiation.

Master these four skills to transform debates into meaningful progress and resolutions.
