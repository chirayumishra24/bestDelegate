Research Foundation

Understand agenda + country stance deeply before writing

Classic Structure

Clear introduction → body → conclusion

T3

START Formula

Statement → Topic → Audience → Reason → Preview

T4

CIA Method

Clash → Information → Action

Hook + Point + CTA

Strong opening hook → clear point → call to action (0:29–1:54 timing sweet spot)

T6

Evidence & Support

Always back arguments with relevant facts, data, statistics, and real-world examples

Mastering speech writing and delivery is essential for successful Model UN participation — it enables delegates to communicate ideas clearly and persuasively in a diplomatic setting.

Delivering Speeches

D1

Engage the Audience

Use eye contact, purposeful gestures, varied tone & pace to connect with every delegate.

D2

Practice Extensively

Rehearse repeatedly to achieve clarity, confidence, and perfect timing under pressure.

D3

Stay Adaptable

Listen actively and respond intelligently to points raised by other delegates.

D4

Maintain Formal Decorum

Always address the Chair properly and treat every delegate with diplomatic respect.

D5

Maximize Impact

Keep every speech concise, focused, and free of repetition — every word must advance your position.

Benefits and Tips

B1

Core Benefits

Speeches build consensus, credibility, and directly shape committee direction.

B2

Powerful Hook

Start with a question, quote, statistic, or anecdote to instantly capture attention.

B3

Use Notes Wisely

Treat your position paper as a guide — speak naturally, not like reading a script.

B4

Practice to Reduce Anxiety

Regular rehearsal dramatically lowers nervousness and boosts flow, timing & confidence.

Mastering speech crafting and delivery is essential — it empowers delegates to communicate ideas clearly, persuasively, and diplomatically.

Key Learning Points

Crafting effective speeches requires research, structure (START/CIA/Hook-Point-Action), and strong supporting facts.

Delivery depends on engagement (eye contact, gestures, tone), practice, adaptability, and formal decorum.

Great speeches turn preparation into influence — they build credibility, shape debates, and drive committee success.
