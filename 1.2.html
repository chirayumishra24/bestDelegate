Chapter 1.2: Roles, Ethics, and Protocols of a Delegate

Role of a Delegate

Key Responsibilities of a Model UN Delegate

Being an effective delegate requires preparation, strategy, collaboration, and professionalism.

🔍

Thorough Research of Your Country’s Position

Before the conference even begins, you must deeply understand your assigned country’s official stance, historical votes, treaties, and national interests on every agenda topic. This is the foundation of credible and persuasive diplomacy.

Good research includes:

  • Official government statements & foreign ministry website
  • UN voting records & past resolutions
  • News articles & think-tank reports from your country’s perspective
  • Alliances and rivalries that influence your position

Delegates who clearly know “why” their country thinks a certain way speak with much more authority.

🗣️

Active Participation in Debate & Public Speaking

Deliver structured speeches, respond to questions, raise points of information, and remain visible throughout formal debate sessions. Consistent participation builds influence and shows engagement.

Tips for strong speeches:

  • Start with a strong hook (statistic, quote, or question)
  • Clearly state your country’s position early
  • Use diplomatic language — avoid aggression
  • End with a call for cooperation or specific action
📝

Collaborative Resolution Drafting

Work together with other delegates — especially potential allies — to write clear, realistic, and politically viable draft resolutions or working papers that address the committee’s agenda.

Effective resolutions usually:

  • Start with preambulatory clauses (justification)
  • Contain operative clauses (actionable steps)
  • Balance ambition with political feasibility
  • Gain broad co-sponsorship before submission
🎭

Accurate Country Representation (Role-Play Integrity)

Always speak and vote in line with your assigned country’s real foreign policy — even when you personally disagree. Authenticity earns respect and makes your arguments more convincing.

Common mistake to avoid:

“I think we should do X because it’s morally right” → instead say “My country believes X because it aligns with our national security / economic interests / historical commitments…”

🔄

Engagement Across All Stages

Participate actively in moderated debate, unmoderated caucuses, resolution writing, amendment negotiations, voting procedure, and follow-up discussions.

Unmoderated caucuses (informal lobbying) are often where most real negotiation and alliance-building happens.

🌐

Consensus Building & Alliance Formation

Use both formal motions and informal conversations to create blocs, negotiate compromises, merge drafts, and gain majority/minimum support for your proposals.

Strong delegates rarely win alone — they build coalitions early and adapt when necessary.

🛡️

Uphold Professionalism & Diplomatic Conduct

Maintain respect, use polite & diplomatic language, listen actively, follow rules of decorum, and treat every delegate with courtesy — regardless of disagreement.

Remember: In Model UN — and real diplomacy — how you behave often matters more than what you say.

“Diplomacy is the art of letting someone else have your way.” – Daniele Vare

A delegate must separate personal beliefs from the position of the country they represent — embodying professionalism, fairness, and diplomatic courtesy.

MUN Delegate Codex

1

Diplomatic Courtesy

Respectful language • no personal attacks • calm under pressure

2

Fairness & Compromise

Open to negotiation • willing to find middle ground • listens actively

3

Procedural Integrity

Respects Rules of Procedure • follows time limits • uses motions correctly

4

Thorough Preparation

Researches country policy • studies agenda topics • knows historical positions

5

Proper Speaking Protocol

Speaks only when recognized • yields time correctly • no interruptions

6

Respectful Interaction

Addresses Chair properly • thanks delegates • stays courteous in disagreement

7

Precision & Clarity

Every speech, motion, clause and amendment should be crystal-clear, concise and strategically worded.

📌 Main Submitter Owns the draft • defends it • leads lobbying
🤝 Co-Submitter Supports drafting • promotes • adds credibility

Additional Delegate Roles in Resolution Writing

📌

Main Submitter / Sponsor

Leads the drafting process, defends the resolution during debate, answers questions, and takes primary responsibility for its content and promotion.

🤝

Co-Submitter / Signatory

Supports the main submitter by contributing ideas, helping refine language, lobbying for votes, and standing up to defend the resolution when needed.

“

Exhibit leadership by guiding collaborations and influencing committee direction diplomatically — balancing assertiveness with respect to gain trust and votes.

Diplomatic Surprise!

Joke: Why do Model UN delegates make terrible secret agents? Because they can't keep anything "classified" — everything ends up in a resolution!

Fun Fact: The term "delegate" comes from Latin "delegatus" meaning "sent" or "appointed" — exactly what MUN delegates are: official representatives of their nation!
