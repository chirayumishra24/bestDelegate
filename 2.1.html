Mastering Your Country Profile

 
1

Understanding Your Country Profile

🏛️

Core Country Knowledge

Study political system, history, economy, culture, and key international relationships.

🌍

Current Stance & Alliances

Know your government’s positions on major global issues and existing diplomatic alliances.

🎯

National Interests & Priorities

Identify what your country cares most about regarding the specific conference agenda topics.

2

Researching Agenda Topics

📚

Background Guides & UN Docs

Start with official background guides and relevant United Nations documents.

🕰️

Historical & Current Context

Understand historical background, recent developments, and current key challenges.

🔗

Positions & Potential Alliances

Map out other countries’ positions to predict conflicts, allies, and coalition opportunities.

3

Gathering Reliable Sources

✅

Credible & Authoritative Sources

Official government sites • UN reports • reputable news • academic journals

📁

Organized Research System

Keep a well-structured binder or digital folder with clear source citations.

Continuous Updates

Monitor news and developments right up to — and during — the conference.

4

Synthesis & Strategic Preparation

🧠

Develop Coherent Arguments

Turn research into logical, policy-aligned arguments.

Propose Realistic Solutions

Suggest diplomacy that serves national interests while remaining achievable.

Anticipate & Counter

Predict opposition arguments and prepare strong, evidence-based responses.

This chapter establishes the foundation for effective delegation by providing a detailed understanding of your country’s position and the issues to be debated — essential for credible and strategic participation in MUN.

Diplomatic Surprise!

Joke: Why do MUN delegates never play hide and seek? Because good delegates are impossible to "block" — they always find a way to speak!

Fun Fact: In 1951, the first-ever Security Council simulation in MUN history ended with a real diplomatic crisis — the chair had to call a recess to calm everyone down!
