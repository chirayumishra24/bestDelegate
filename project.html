Capstone Project: Full MUN Committee Simulation

⭐ Capstone Project Overview

This is the final challenge of the entire MUN Training Program.

You will simulate a complete Model UN committee session from start to finish — just like a real conference.

Prove you can research, speak, negotiate, draft resolutions, lead, and represent your country like a true Best Delegate.

Submit the link or video recording of your full simulation on LMS

🎯 Learning Objectives

By completing this capstone you will demonstrate mastery of:

Deep country & topic research
Writing strong position papers & resolutions
Delivering confident, persuasive speeches
Effective argumentation, rebuttal & diplomacy
Coalition building, negotiation & compromise
Leading committee, drafting amendments & passing resolutions

📋 What You Must Submit

1. Choose a Real MUN Committee & Agenda

  • Example: UNGA Third Committee – Elimination of Racism
  • Or: UNSC – Situation in the Middle East
  • Use real past or current UN agenda topics

2. Research & Prepare (Submit these files)

  • Position Paper (1–2 pages)
  • Delegate Research File (key facts, allies, interests)
  • Opening Speech script (1–2 min)

3. Record or Document the Simulation

  • Video: Record yourself giving speeches, negotiating, drafting (Zoom/Google Meet with friends or solo role-play)
  • OR Written Log: Detailed script of what you said + resolutions/amendments you proposed
  • Minimum: 15–20 minutes of active participation

4. Final Output

  • Passed resolution or amendment (even if mock)
  • Reflection: 300–500 words on what you learned, challenges faced, how you applied course concepts

Pro Tips to Score Full Marks

  • Use real country policy — never invent facts
  • Show diplomacy: compromise, build coalitions, avoid aggression
  • Include procedural language: “The delegation of… moves to…”
  • Time yourself — speeches should be 1–2 min max
  • Take screenshots or clips of resolution drafting
  • Reflect honestly: what went well? what would you improve?
