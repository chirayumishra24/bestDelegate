Chapter 2.3: Building a Delegate Research File

Delegate Research Cockpit Mission-critical components & organization protocol

 

CORE HUB

Your country's identity + agenda topics form the nucleus. Everything else radiates from here.

C1

Country Profile

Name, geography, government structure, key allies & neighbors, recent political/economic developments

C2

Topic Background

Global/regional context, statistics, definitions, recent news & developments

C3

Past International Action

UN resolutions, treaties, conventions, your country’s voting record & participation

C4

Country Policy

Official statements, speeches, foreign ministry documents, policy papers

C5

Possible Solutions

Think-tank ideas, expert papers, op-eds + your own realistic proposals

C6

Position Papers & Working Notes

Drafts, working papers, personal notes for speeches, resolutions, amendments & strategy

Best Practices – Insight Protocol

P1

Clear Sectioning

Physical binder or digital folder with clearly labeled sections/tabs

P2

Primary Sources First

Prioritize UN docs, government statements, official reports over secondary sources

P3

Live Notes Zone

Dedicated blank pages/digital section for real-time ideas & notes during committee

P4

Dynamic & Continuously Updated

Refresh file regularly as new developments occur — before and during conference

 
CLASSIFIED INSIGHT

This structure ensures delegates are well-prepared to contribute meaningfully to debates, negotiate resolutions, and represent their country’s interests effectively.

Key Learning Points

✦ A Delegate Research File is your secret weapon — a structured, organized collection of facts, policies, and ideas for confident participation.

✦ Include all key sections: Country Profile, Topic Background, Past Action, Country Policy, Solutions, and Notes.

Build it early, update it constantly, and use it actively — it's the foundation of strong speeches, resolutions, and diplomacy.
